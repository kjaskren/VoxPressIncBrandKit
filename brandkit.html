Who we are
and why we exist.

VOX Press is a 501(c)(3) literary arts and prison education nonprofit in Oxford, Mississippi. Since 2004 we have published experimental literature, hosted the Artists Series, and run the only creative writing program inside Mississippi's prison system.

Mission Encourage, promote, and create opportunities for literature and art by communities mainstream publishing has ignored.
Founded 2004 as a literary journal · Incorporated 2007 · Oxford, Mississippi
Status IRS-recognized 501(c)(3) nonprofit · All donations tax-deductible
Positioning Statement
For writers and readers who believe every voice deserves a page, VOX Press is the literary nonprofit that gives a platform to voices mainstream publishing has never had room for — not as charity, but as art.
Key Differentiators
Only statewide creative writing program inside Mississippi prisons
Publishes avant-garde & experimental — not commercial — literature
20+ year track record; two gubernatorial-level awards
Rooted in Mississippi, informed by a national literary tradition

03 · Brand Personality
04 / 16
Six words that guide every decision.
If a design, headline, or campaign doesn't feel like at least three of these, revisit it.
Literary
Not academic
We take language seriously. Our writing and design reflect the care of a careful editor, not a corporate marketing team.
"30+ essays celebrating the spectacular life of Barry Hannah."
Dignified
Not pitying
We describe the people we serve as writers and artists — never as victims or charity cases. Dignity is non-negotiable.
"A collection of writings from over 30 Mississippi inmates" — not "heartbreaking stories from behind bars."
Urgent
Not alarmist
This work matters. We communicate that plainly, without manufactured crisis or emotional manipulation.
"Nobody is discovering VOX Press for the first time through Google. Fixing simple things would change that."
Rooted
Not provincial
Mississippi isn't a limitation — it's a vantage point. We are proudly specific in place while globally relevant in subject.
"Oxford, Mississippi · EST. 2004"
Uncompromising
Not inaccessible
We publish avant-garde and experimental work. We don't soften it for mass appeal — but we explain it with warmth.
"Anti-poetry that challenges what a poem can be."
Inclusive
Not preachy
We show inclusion through action: who we publish, who we teach, who we platform. We don't lecture about it.
"Every voice deserves a page." (shown once — then proven by the catalog)

Usage Rules
80 / 20 Ratio
Cream and White make up 80% of any surface. Ink and Slate are used for text and accent sections — never as the default background.
Never Compete with Gold
When Gold appears on a surface, keep surrounding text in Ink or Cream. Don't place coloured text next to a gold element — it dilutes impact.
Slate vs. Ink
Use Slate (#1E2A38) for large dark sections — it reads warmer and more inviting. Use Ink (#0F0E0E) only for text and micro-details.
Warm Cream for Contrast
Cream Warm (#F3EDE3) creates gentle depth between sections. Alternate with Cream (#FAF7F2) to avoid visual flatness.

06 · Logo
10 / 16
Primary wordmark.

The VOX Press wordmark is always set in Playfair Display. "VOX" in Gold at 900 weight; "Press, Inc." in Ink Soft at 400 weight with generous letter-spacing.

Clear Space = cap-height of "V"
VOX
Press, Inc.
Amplifying overlooked voices.
Logo Rules
📐
Clear Space: Always maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the "V" on all four sides of the wordmark. Nothing may intrude into this zone.
📏
Minimum Size: Digital minimum is 120px wide. Print minimum is 1 inch wide. Below these sizes, drop "Press, Inc." and use "VOX" alone.
🎨
Gold on "VOX": The "VOX" element is always rendered in Gold (#B8892A) or Gold Light (#D4A84E on dark backgrounds). It is never rendered in any other color.
↔️
Proportion Lock: Never stretch, condense, or alter the width-to-height ratio of the wordmark. Scale uniformly only.
✍️
Tagline is optional: "Amplifying overlooked voices." is used beneath the wordmark when space allows — minimum 180px width. Never required.

06 · Logo — Don'ts
12 / 16
What the logo is never allowed to do.
These rules protect the authority and legibility of the mark across all applications.
VOX
Don't stretch or distort
Altering the proportions breaks the font's optical balance. Scale uniformly only.
VOX
Don't use Gold on Gold Pale
This pairing fails WCAG contrast. The wordmark disappears against a warm background.
VOX
Don't recolor "VOX"
"VOX" in any color other than Gold, Gold Light, Ink, or White is off-brand. No exceptions.
VOX
Don't change the typeface
The wordmark is always Playfair Display. Setting it in Inter, Arial, or any other font is not permitted.
VOX
Don't use as a watermark
The logo should never appear at reduced opacity as a background element. Use the bg-text pattern instead.
VOX
Don't place on busy backgrounds
The logo requires a clean, solid background. Textured or patterned backgrounds compromise legibility.
07 · Voice & Tone
13 / 16
How VOX Press speaks.

Voice is consistent — it's who we are. Tone shifts — it's how we feel in a given context. These four principles govern both.

PRINCIPLE 01
Lead with the work, not the cause.
We describe what we've published, who we've platformed, and what we've built — not what we "believe in." Our actions make the argument; our copy just introduces them.
"Unit 29 documents writings from over 30 men in Mississippi's most brutal prison unit." — Not: "We believe in the power of voices behind bars."
PRINCIPLE 02
Name the place. Name the people.
We are specific. Mississippi, Oxford, Parchman, Lafayette County. We never use vague language like "underserved communities" without immediately saying who and where.
"The only creative writing program inside Mississippi's prison system" — Not: "programs serving vulnerable populations nationwide."
PRINCIPLE 03
Write like an editor, not a marketer.
Clean sentences. No jargon. No buzzwords. No exclamation points except in dialogue or headlines that genuinely warrant them. Our prose should feel like it was written by someone who reads.
"A one-of-a-kind compilation on avant-garde music over the past five decades." — Specific, precise, no hype.
PRINCIPLE 04
Dignity is not optional.
Everyone VOX Press writes about — incarcerated writers, emerging authors, community students — is referred to with the same dignity we would give any published author. Never "inmates" as a label of identity. Always "writers," "students," "contributors."
"Writers at Parchman Prison" — Not: "inmates participating in our program."
